In this study, the 3-axis accelerometer signal acquired from the foot was denoised using a low-pass FIR (finite impulse response) filter. Using fundamental frequency analysis, the dominant frequency was identified and used to determine an optimal window length for sliding across the signal. The turning region was then extracted using the Pearson correlation coefficient between overlapping segments generated by shifting the selected window across the full signal. After identifying the walking segments, stride time was calculated using a simple peak-picking algorithm. The stance and swing periods of the pseudo-steps, obtained through a double-step time calculation algorithm, were estimated using dynamic time warping. Finally, the stance phase ratio, defined as the percentage of a step occupied by the stance phase, was computed. The results were compared against the APDM system, yielding a mean absolute error of 0.029 s for stride time and 0.0084 for stance phase ratio.
